## The Two Components of Solar Gravity

In Cartesian Physics, solar gravity has two fundamental components:
1. **Displacement** - the pushing or pulling force
2. **Rotation** - the spinning or orbital motion

Understanding how different physics systems handle these components reveals why modern physics has fundamental problems—and how Descartes' original framework provides the solution.

## Newton's System: Linear Inertia

Newton divided gravity into:
- **"G" (gravitational constant)** - handles displacement
- **Inertia** - handles motion resistance

**The critical flaw**: Newton's inertia is **linear**, not rotational.

This causes errors in:
- Mercury's precession (orbital shift)
- Gravitational lensing (light bending)

Newton's framework works for simple cases but fails when rotation becomes significant.

## Einstein's Fix: Warping Spacetime

Einstein attempted to correct Newton's errors by splitting gravity differently:
- **Gravitational mass** - handles displacement
- **Warping of spacetime** - handles rotation via inertial mass

This warping effectively fixes Newton's linear inertia problem by making space itself curve, which accounts for rotational effects.

**Einstein's innovation**: Space warps, and objects follow the curves—rotation is built into the geometry itself.

## Einstein's Fatal Flaws

However, Einstein's warping of spacetime is **disproven** by multiple phenomena:

1. **Singularities** - Mathematics breaks down, producing infinite densities
2. **Massless galactic gravity** - Dark matter needed to explain galaxy rotation (matter we can't detect)
3. **Expansion of the universe** - Space itself expanding contradicts static geometry
4. **Black hole information paradox** - Information appears to be destroyed, violating quantum mechanics

These aren't minor issues—they reveal fundamental problems with the spacetime warping concept itself.

## The Cartesian Solution: Vortex Rotation

We fix Einstein's problems by **replacing warping with vortex rotation** of massless aethereal space particles from Descartes' original framework.

**Key components:**

### 1. Massless Aether Particles
Instead of warping spacetime, gravity operates through rotating vortices of massless aether particles. These create the displacement and rotational effects without requiring space itself to bend.

### 2. Spin of Masses
We add **spin to masses** to provide the extra push observed in:
- Mercury's precession
- The 1919 Eddington experiment (gravitational lensing)

The spin of bodies contributes to the overall vortex dynamics, explaining the additional effects that neither Newton nor Einstein could account for without adjustment.

### 3. Relational Spin
These spins are **relational**, not absolute. This replaces:
- Einstein's Relativity
- Mach's Principle

With **Descartes' spin relations**, as he wrote in *Principia Philosophiae* Part 4, Article 202:

> "I deny the existence of gravity inside any body. Gravity is a quality that depends on the relations of situation and motion of bodies on each other."

Gravity is not a property of individual bodies but emerges from the **relational dynamics** between spinning bodies within the aether vortex.
